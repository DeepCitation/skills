# Canada Life — Attending Physician Statement (LTD)

Canada Life's Attending Physician Statement for long-term disability claims. Canada Life (formerly Great-West Life) is one of Canada's largest group benefit insurers.

## Insurer-Specific Notes

- **Form title**: "Attending Physician Statement" — same naming as Manulife
- **Hybrid format**: Canada Life uses a mix of checkboxes and narrative fields — more structured than Sun Life, less rigid than Manulife
- **Occupation analysis**: Canada Life places significant weight on the physician's understanding of the patient's job demands
- **Concurrent conditions**: Canada Life explicitly asks physicians to list ALL conditions affecting function, not just the primary disability

## Section Structure

1. **Patient and Policy Information** — name, DOB, employer, policy number, occupation, date last worked
2. **Diagnosis** — primary and all concurrent diagnoses with ICD-10 codes, dates of onset
3. **Objective Findings** — clinical exam findings, diagnostic results, mental status if applicable
4. **Treatment Plan** — current medications with dosages, therapies, surgeries (completed and planned), specialist referrals
5. **Functional Assessment** — physical demands checklist (sedentary through heavy) plus narrative for cognitive/emotional limitations
6. **Occupational Impact** — specific restrictions and limitations relative to the patient's job duties
7. **Prognosis** — expected duration, improvement/stability/deterioration, estimated return-to-work date

## Common Pitfalls (Canada Life–Specific)

- Not listing concurrent conditions — Canada Life uses the full clinical picture for their assessment; a single diagnosis may not justify the claimed level of disability
- Answering the occupational impact section generically — Canada Life adjudicators compare the physician's restrictions against the employer's job description
- Omitting mental health assessment for physical conditions — Canada Life frequently asks "is there a psychological component?" and an omission triggers follow-up

## Terminology

- "Plan Member" = the insured employee
- "Group Policyholder" = the employer
- "Disability Case Manager" = Canada Life's assigned case manager
- Note: Forms may still reference "Great-West Life" — Canada Life rebranded from Great-West Life in 2023
