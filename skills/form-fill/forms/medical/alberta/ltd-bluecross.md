# Alberta Blue Cross — Initial Disability Insurance Medical Statement (LTD)

This form is Alberta Blue Cross's insurer-specific physician statement for long-term disability claims. It follows the general LTD structure (see `ltd.md`) but has Blue Cross–specific field labels, ordering, and expectations.

## Insurer-Specific Notes

- **Form title**: "Initial Disability Insurance Medical Statement" (Blue Cross does not use "Physician's Statement" wording)
- **Two-part form**: Part A is completed by the employee; Part B (this form) by the attending physician
- **Functional capacity emphasis**: Blue Cross weighs functional limitation descriptions heavily — narrative answers matter more than checkboxes
- **Return-to-work plan required**: Blue Cross expects a graduated return-to-work plan or explicit statement of why one is not feasible

## Section Structure

Follows the same section pattern as the generic LTD spec (`ltd.md`), with these Blue Cross–specific additions:

1. **Patient and Policy Information** — same as generic LTD
2. **Diagnosis** — Blue Cross requires both ICD-10 codes AND narrative diagnosis descriptions
3. **Treatment and Medications** — includes a specific field for "date treatment began" per condition
4. **Functional Limitations** — Blue Cross uses a detailed functional capacity grid (sitting, standing, walking, lifting, cognitive) with hourly tolerances
5. **Prognosis and Return to Work** — requires specific dates, not ranges; "unable to estimate" triggers additional medical advisor review

## Common Pitfalls (Blue Cross–Specific)

- Leaving the functional capacity grid blank and only writing narrative — Blue Cross adjudicators use the grid for their initial assessment
- Not specifying hourly tolerances (e.g., "can sit for 20 minutes" vs. just "limited sitting")
- Missing the "date treatment began" field — Blue Cross uses this to assess treatment timeline adequacy

## Eligibility Criteria

Same as generic LTD — "own occupation" for first 24 months, then "any occupation." See `ltd.md` for full criteria.

## Terminology

- "Member" = the insured employee (not "patient" or "claimant" in Blue Cross forms)
- "Plan Sponsor" = the employer
- "Disability Management Consultant" = Blue Cross's case manager assigned to the claim
