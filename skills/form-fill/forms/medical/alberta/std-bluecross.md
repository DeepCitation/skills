# Alberta Blue Cross — Initial Disability Insurance Medical Statement (STD)

This form is Alberta Blue Cross's insurer-specific physician statement for short-term disability claims. It uses the same "Initial Disability Insurance Medical Statement" form as LTD but is submitted during the STD benefit period.

## Insurer-Specific Notes

- **Same form as LTD**: Blue Cross uses one form for both STD and LTD — the benefit period determines which program applies
- **STD focus**: During the STD period, Blue Cross expects recovery-oriented language and concrete return-to-work dates
- **Elimination period**: Typically 0–14 days for STD under Blue Cross group plans

## Section Structure

Same as `ltd-bluecross.md`. The key difference is in how the physician frames answers:

- **Prognosis**: Frame in weeks, not months. Include a specific expected return-to-work date.
- **Functional limitations**: Describe current state AND expected trajectory ("currently unable to sit >15 min; expect improvement to 30 min within 4 weeks")
- **Treatment**: Emphasize active, time-limited interventions (physio series, medication trial with review date)

## Common Pitfalls (STD-Specific)

- Using LTD-style language about permanence or long-term disability on an STD claim
- Open-ended return-to-work estimates — Blue Cross STD adjudicators expect specific dates
- Not mentioning graduated return-to-work as an option

## Terminology

Same as `ltd-bluecross.md` — "Member," "Plan Sponsor," "Disability Management Consultant."
