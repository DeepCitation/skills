# Manulife — Attending Physician Statement (LTD)

Manulife's Attending Physician Statement (APS) is the standard form for long-term disability claims under Manulife group insurance plans. Manulife is one of Canada's largest group insurers.

## Insurer-Specific Notes

- **Form title**: "Attending Physician Statement" (APS) — Manulife's standard name
- **Structured format**: Manulife's APS is more structured than some insurers, with specific checkboxes and grids rather than open narrative fields
- **Cognitive/psychological section**: Manulife includes a dedicated mental health functional assessment section, even for physical disability claims
- **Drug therapy detail**: Manulife requests detailed medication history including start dates, dosage changes, and reasons for changes

## Section Structure

1. **Patient Information** — name, DOB, occupation, employer, policy number
2. **Diagnosis** — primary and secondary with ICD-10, date of onset, date first seen for this condition
3. **Objective Findings** — physical exam findings, diagnostic test results, mental status exam if applicable
4. **Treatment** — medications with full history (drug, dose, start date, response), therapies, surgical procedures
5. **Functional Abilities** — checkbox grid for physical demands (sedentary/light/medium/heavy) plus cognitive assessment
6. **Prognosis** — expected duration of disability, expected return-to-work date, whether condition is likely to improve/stabilize/deteriorate

## Common Pitfalls (Manulife-Specific)

- Not completing the cognitive/psychological section — Manulife flags incomplete APS forms for follow-up, delaying the claim
- Listing medications without the dosage change history — Manulife uses titration history to assess treatment adequacy
- Checking "heavy" physical demand limitation without supporting objective findings — triggers medical advisor review

## Terminology

- "Plan Member" = the insured employee
- "Plan Sponsor" = the employer
- "APS" = Attending Physician Statement
- "Rehabilitation Consultant" = Manulife's return-to-work case manager
