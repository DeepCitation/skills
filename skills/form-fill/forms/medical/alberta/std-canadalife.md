# Canada Life — Attending Physician Statement (STD)

Canada Life uses the same Attending Physician Statement for STD and LTD. During the STD benefit period, the form should emphasize acute onset, active treatment, and return-to-work planning.

## Insurer-Specific Notes

- **Same form as LTD**: Canada Life's APS is shared across benefit periods
- **STD focus**: Recovery-oriented answers, timeline in weeks, specific return-to-work date
- **Elimination period**: Typically 0–14 days under Canada Life group STD plans

## Section Structure

Same as `ltd-canadalife.md`. STD-specific framing:

- **Prognosis**: Recovery timeline in weeks. Must include return-to-work date.
- **Functional assessment**: Current limitations AND expected improvement — "restricted to sedentary work for 4 weeks, then light duties for 2 weeks"
- **Concurrent conditions**: Still required — Canada Life assesses the full picture even for STD

## Common Pitfalls (STD-Specific)

- Framing the condition as chronic or permanent on an STD claim
- Not providing modified duties guidance — Canada Life actively promotes graduated return-to-work
- Missing return-to-work date — mandatory for STD processing

## Terminology

Same as `ltd-canadalife.md`. Note possible "Great-West Life" branding on older forms.
